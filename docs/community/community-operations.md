Between large events, the community needs continuous heartbeat activities to keep people engaged, share knowledge, and build relationships. These ongoing programs turn one-time event attendees into long-term community participants and eventually champions.

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:handshake" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Community Engagement and Programming

### Regular Engagement Activities

- **Office hours and Q&A clinics:** Schedule weekly or biweekly drop-in calls where makers can ask questions. Rotate champion hosts so it's not always the core team answering.

- **Discussion forums:** Keep an active Viva Engage or Teams channel. Encourage interaction with polls, challenges, and quick tips. The goal is to move from core team announcements to open discussions.

- **Mentoring and peer support:** Create a formal mentorship program. Pair new makers with experienced makers or champions for 4–6 weeks. Provide a mentorship guide with meeting suggestions.

- **Gamification and challenges:** Introduce friendly competitions or badges. Run month-long "agent challenges" where members earn points for tasks like attending an event, posting a tip, or publishing an agent in production.

- **Content spotlights:** Host 15- to 30-minute mini events where a member shares a tip or gives a quick demo. This disseminates knowledge and builds presenter confidence.

### Encourage Champions to Lead

- Have champions **host office hours** or **moderate the forum** for a week
- Hold a monthly or bimonthly **champion roundtable** to share feedback, coordinate events, and discuss advanced subjects
- This distributes workload and empowers champions

### Keep It Fun and Rewarding

- Start meetings with "Agent Trivia" or community memes
- Use reaction features (Likes, Praise badges in Teams) generously
- When someone contributes, suggest they turn that contribution into a short demo or blog

### Connection to Events

- Before hackathons: Run prep sessions and team matchmaking posts
- After events: Use the forum for follow-up discussions and continued project refinement
- Answer unaddressed Q&A questions in the forum with tags

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:chart-increasing" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Measurement, Feedback, and Impact

### Event Metrics

For each event, capture:
- **Attendance:** Registrants versus actual attendees and turnout percentage
- **Participant profile:** Breakdown by role or department
- **Engagement during event:** Questions asked, poll responses, hands-on completion rates
- **Feedback and satisfaction:** Post-event survey via Microsoft Forms — overall satisfaction (1–5), NPS, most valuable session, improvement suggestions
- **Immediate outcomes:** "Do you plan to start an agent project in the next month?"

### Community Metrics

Continuously monitor:
- **Membership and growth:** Total members, growth trajectory, conversion rate of event attendees to community members
- **Activity and engagement:** Monthly active users in forums, event participation trends
- **Champion and skill growth:** Number of champions and active mentors, makers at each skill level
- **Solution impact:** Count of agents deployed, key results (time saved, satisfaction, error reduction)
- **Adoption indicators:** Growth in active Copilot Studio users or published agents in the tenant

### Feedback Loops

- Post-event **Forms or quick polls**
- Periodic **pulse surveys** gauging member value, challenges, and support quality
- **Analyze popular content** to inform what to produce more of

### Report and Iterate

- Create a **Power BI dashboard** pulling from various sources for a live view of community health
- Share a **quarterly community report** with leadership pairing metrics with narratives
- Treat community initiatives like a product in continuous improvement mode
