# <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:handshake" width="36" height="36" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.3em;"></iconify-icon> Best Practices for Establishing AI Communities

Technology adoption is a "people movement" as much as a technology rollout. By investing in your people through a strong community and well-run events, you ensure that Copilot Studio isn't just deployed — it's embraced, used to its full potential, and continuously delivers value across your enterprise.

This framework is designed for internal adoption leads, champions, and community organizers who want to:

- Launch and scale Copilot Studio adoption across their organization
- Build a self-sustaining community of makers, mentors, and champions
- Deliver repeatable, high-quality events that drive real business outcomes
- Align Copilot Studio efforts with strategic goals and measurable value

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:people-hugging" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Build Your Community: Ten Strategic Focus Areas

1. **Community purpose and strategic alignment** — Define the vision, mission, and goals for the community, aligned to business priorities and AI adoption objectives.

2. **Community leadership and governance** — Establish a core team with defined roles to steer community operations and run events effectively.

3. **Maker enablement and training lifecycle** — Implement phased training (onboarding, skill building, advanced training) tailored to different maker personas.

4. **Event design and execution** — Use standardized event templates and content modules to deliver high-quality, repeatable events like meetups, workshops, hackathons, and bootcamps.

5. **Community engagement and programming** — Maintain momentum with regular activities like Q&As, challenges, mentoring, and forums. Involve champions to deepen ongoing participation.

6. **Measurement, feedback, and impact** — Track event-level and community-level metrics such as attendance, satisfaction, skills growth, agent adoption, and business value.

7. **Infrastructure and tooling** — Use Microsoft tools like Viva Engage, SharePoint, Teams, and Forms to enable communication, content sharing, and event logistics.

8. **Partnerships and amplification** — Partner with internal teams (IT, Security, Analytics, Business Units) and external communities (Microsoft user groups, MVP networks).

9. **Recognition and success sharing** — Celebrate wins such as event outcomes, successful agents, and maker achievements. Spotlight champions and tell success stories.

10. **Sustainability and growth** — Employ a maturity model for community roles and a flywheel strategy (events → champions → adoption → more events) to evolve the community.
