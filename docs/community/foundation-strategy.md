By clearly defining the community's purpose and aligning it with strategic objectives, you create a "north star" that guides all activities. This clarity ensures that every event agenda, training module, and discussion thread is purposeful and drives toward common goals.

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:bullseye" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Community Purpose and Strategic Alignment

### Define a Clear Mission

Start by articulating why your Copilot Studio community exists, and ensure every event or activity ties back to that purpose.

{: .note }
> **Example mission statement:** "We empower employees to build AI agents with Copilot Studio, accelerating automation and innovation across our organization."

The mission statement should reflect how the community and its events support the company's broader goals — improving productivity, customer experience, or data-driven decision-making.

### Set Specific Goals

Break the mission into measurable objectives:

- **Adoption goals:** e.g., "Train 100 new makers in Copilot Studio basics in the next six months"
- **Engagement goals:** e.g., "Achieve 50% active participation in the community forum"
- **Event goals:** e.g., "Host at least one Copilot Studio event per quarter with an average satisfaction score of 4.5/5"
- **Impact goals:** e.g., "Automate processes saving 500 employee hours through AI agents by year-end"

### Align with Business Priorities

Ensure community goals correspond to leadership's objectives. If the company's strategic priority is improving customer service, focus community energy on agents that assist support teams. Highlight metrics like "an agent that reduced customer response time by 40%."

### Key Questions to Guide Strategy

- **"Who are our makers?"** — Identify your audience, including departments, roles, and skill levels
- **"How can we help them succeed?"** — Determine what training, support, and resources makers need
- **"How can we recognize their efforts?"** — Plan for celebration and rewards
- **"How can we grow the community?"** — Think about scaling: more events, cross-team collaboration, converting attendees into active members

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:crown" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Community Leadership and Governance

Strong communities have intentional leadership. Assemble a core team to coordinate:

| Role | Responsibility |
|:-----|:--------------|
| **Community Lead (Program Manager)** | Overall direction. Aligns activities with business goals, secures leadership support, oversees progress. Ensures a positive, inclusive environment. |
| **Content & Communications Lead** | Curates content, manages the SharePoint/Viva Engage hub, coordinates newsletters and intranet posts. Documents best practices and FAQ guides. |
| **Event Lead** | Plans and executes events from small lunch-and-learns to large hackathons. Handles scheduling, speaker coordination, and post-event follow-up. Tracks event metrics. |
| **Champion Coordinator** | Recruits and supports champions in different departments. Relays feedback, organizes champion meetups, recognizes contributions. |
| **Executive Sponsor** | Advocates at the leadership level. Helps secure funding, participates in key events, lends credibility. |

### Practical Tips

- **Start with available resources:** Split work as needed. Add roles as the community grows.
- **Regular coordination:** Hold biweekly or monthly core team check-ins. Use a shared planning board.
- **Governance documentation:** Develop a community governance charter including team, event cadence, guidelines, and code of conduct.
- **Center of Excellence collaboration:** Engage your CoE if one exists. Use CoE resources and make lessons flow bidirectionally.
