To turn curious employees into confident Copilot Studio makers, provide a structured learning journey that meets them at their skill level and guides them to proficiency.

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:graduation-cap" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Maker Enablement and Training Lifecycle

### Maker Personas

Tailor training to different backgrounds:

- **Citizen developer / business user:** Typically starts with Agent Builder in Microsoft 365 Copilot and moves to Copilot Studio as needs grow. Needs an introduction to AI concepts and reassurance that templates make agent creation accessible without deep coding skills.

- **Data / AI specialist, power user:** Primarily uses Copilot Studio for deeper configuration and integration. Needs an overview of Power Platform basics and understanding of how AI expertise applies within Copilot Studio.

- **IT professional / developer:** Core Copilot Studio persona focused on enterprise integration, governance, and scale. Needs clear guidance on architecture, security, compliance, and advanced integration.

### Phased Training Program

**Phase 1 — Onboarding (Awareness → Basic understanding):**
- Introduction to AI agents and Copilot Studio capabilities
- Basic platform navigation and first agent creation
- Understanding governance and security considerations

**Phase 2 — Skill Building (Understanding → Competence):**
- Advanced agent design patterns
- Integration with existing systems and data sources
- Troubleshooting and optimization techniques

**Phase 3 — Advanced Practice (Competence → Proficiency):**
- Complex scenario development
- Mentoring other makers
- Contributing to community knowledge base

### Reinforcement Mechanisms

- Maintain a **Copilot Studio learning section** on your intranet with links to Microsoft Learn training modules
- Set up a **playlist for recorded trainings**, demo sessions, and external videos
- Develop skills via the [Power Up](https://powerup.microsoft.com/) program
- Attend [Agent in a Day events](https://www.microsoft.com/events/search-catalog?q=Agent+in+a+Day)
- Advanced leads can attend the [Copilot Studio Architecture Bootcamp](https://microsoft.github.io/cat/programs/architecture-bootcamp.html)
- Implement a **"Prompt of the week"** or **"Agent tip of the week"** campaign
- Periodically update training content based on community feedback

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:calendar" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Event Design and Execution

### Link Events to Objectives

| Objective | Event Type | Success Looks Like |
|:----------|:-----------|:------------------|
| **Awareness & Inspiration** | Meetups (1-2 hours) | Attendees leave excited about potential, with ideas for their context |
| **Skill Acquisition** | Workshops or bootcamps | Participants gain hands-on skills with guided lab exercises |
| **Solution Creation** | Hackathons ("Agent-thons") | Teams build prototype agents addressing real problems |
| **Community Building** | Showcase or conference | Teams share work, attend advanced talks, strengthen networks |

### Standardized Event Templates

- **Agenda templates** — Predefined structures for each event type
- **Logistics checklists** — Venue/virtual setup, equipment, feedback channels
- **Speaker and mentor preparation** — Brief roles and expectations
- **Content modules** — Use [hands-on labs](https://github.com/microsoft/mcs-labs) or the [Copilot Studio Agent Academy](https://microsoft.github.io/agent-academy/)
- Always end events with a **call-to-action** bridging to sustained community membership

### Event Format Decision Guide

- **Broad awareness, mixed audience, limited time** → Meetup or town hall
- **Focused training group, half/full day** → Workshop with structured labs
- **Tangible solutions and camaraderie** → Hackathon with IT support
- **Cement community, showcase broad progress** → Summit or conference

### Post-Event Transition

- Funnel participants into the community (Teams/Viva Engage)
- Share a concise event recap with materials and next steps
- For hackathons, follow up on teams' prototype development needs
- Answer unanswered Q&A questions in the forum
