As organizations move beyond experimenting with AI, many struggle to scale AI agents in a way that's secure, measurable, and deeply embedded into how work gets done. Agentic AI adds new capabilities like autonomous decision-making, multistep orchestration, and human-agent collaboration, but it also requires a new enterprise operating model.

The **agentic AI maturity model** provides a structured framework to help you understand where you are in your agent adoption journey and what you need to do next. Rather than focusing solely on technology, the model looks holistically across strategy, process transformation, governance, value realization, architecture, operations, organizational readiness, and responsible AI.

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:compass" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Overview of the Maturity Model

The model is organized into **five maturity levels** and **five capability pillars**. These provide a consistent way to assess your current state, understand progression, and identify where targeted investment will have the greatest impact.

### Maturity Levels

{: .note }
> The agentic AI maturity model is based on the Capability Maturity Model (CMM), widely used in software development, IT, and other industries to assess and enhance organizational maturity.

- **Level 100 — Initial:** Agentic AI initiatives are unplanned and experimental. Capabilities are inconsistent, siloed, and dependent on individuals rather than repeatable practices.
- **Level 200 — Repeatable:** Early patterns and practices begin to emerge. Teams can repeat certain activities, but approaches are still informal and uneven across the organization.
- **Level 300 — Defined:** Capabilities are formally defined, documented, and supported by governance, standards, and operating models. Agentic AI initiatives align more clearly to business goals.
- **Level 400 — Capable:** Agents are embedded into enterprise planning and operations. Processes, governance, and technology support scaling and cross-team collaboration.
- **Level 500 — Efficient:** The organization operates as an agent-first enterprise. Capabilities are optimized, continuously improved, and supported by strong leadership, culture, and trust.

### Capability Pillars

The model evaluates maturity across five pillars, each representing an important dimension of successful AI adoption:

| Pillar | Focus |
|:-------|:------|
| [Pillar 1: AI Strategy & Experience]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/maturity/pillar-1-strategy) | Aligning AI initiatives with business objectives, leadership priorities, and user experience goals |
| [Pillar 2: Business Strategy]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/maturity/pillar-2-business) | Redesigning end-to-end processes for human–agent collaboration, measuring business impact |
| [Pillar 3: AI Governance & Security]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/maturity/pillar-3-governance) | Establishing guardrails, controls, oversight, and lifecycle governance |
| [Pillar 4: Technology & Data]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/maturity/pillar-4-technology) | Building scalable, secure technical foundations and data access patterns |
| [Pillar 5: Organization & Culture]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/maturity/pillar-5-organization) | Enabling people, roles, incentives, and ways of working |

## <iconify-icon icon="fluent-emoji-flat:clipboard" width="24" height="24" style="vertical-align: middle; margin-right: 0.2em;"></iconify-icon> Quick Reference

| Level | AI Strategy & Experience | Business Strategy | Governance & Security | Technology & Data | Organization & Culture |
|:------|:------------------------|:------------------|:---------------------|:------------------|:----------------------|
| **100** | No strategy, siloed experimentation | Human-only workflows, manual processes | No governance, basic compliance | Fragmented tooling, no architecture | No training, isolated pilots |
| **200** | Early vision forming, limited alignment | Pilots improve single steps | Early policies, basic environment separation | Basic environment structure | Sporadic training, informal communities |
| **300** | Formal strategy, cross-functional planning | Documented governance, human-agent teaming | Standardized practices, reusable components | Reusable components, telemetry platforms | Formal enablement, active communities |
| **400** | AI integrated in enterprise planning | Cross-system orchestration, measurable value | Proactive governance with automation | Scalable enterprise foundations | Champions embedded, shared acceleration |
| **500** | AI-first culture, continuous iteration | Adaptive, autonomous processes | Predictive risk management | Advanced multi-agent patterns | Self-sustaining community |
